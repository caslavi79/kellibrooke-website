Overview

Divorce is complicated enough without your mortgage making it worse. Whether you need to refinance your ex off the existing loan, buy a new place while your name is still on the old mortgage, or figure out how a court order affects your qualifying — I deal with these files regularly.

The common scenarios: refinance to remove a former spouse, buy a new home while the divorce decree assigns the old mortgage debt to your ex, use child support or alimony as qualifying income, or sort out the timing of a quit claim deed in relation to the refinance.

Every one of these has specific documentation requirements and timing considerations that most lenders handle poorly. I've done enough of them to know exactly what's needed.

Refinancing off an ex

Buying post-divorce

Timing
When to refinance, when to buy, how to coordinate the sequence. I help plan it all.

How I Handle This

I start by reading the divorce decree or separation agreement. I identify what the lender will need — liability exclusions, quit claim deeds, court-ordered payment history, and any timing requirements.

Then I build a plan that coordinates the mortgage transaction with your legal situation. The goal is a clean transaction with no surprises.

Questions I Get

How do I get my ex off the mortgage?

Refinance the loan in your name only. Your ex signs a quit claim deed releasing their ownership interest. I coordinate the timing so both happen together.

My ex is supposed to pay the old mortgage but it's still in my name. What do I do?

A liability exclusion letter, combined with proof that your ex has been making the payments, can remove that debt from your qualifying calculations. I handle this documentation regularly.

Can I use child support as income?

Yes, if you have a court order and can document a consistent receipt history. The support needs to continue for a sufficient period into the future. I'll verify whether it qualifies.

What if the divorce isn't finalized yet?

You can still buy or refinance in many cases, but the documentation requirements are different. A separation agreement may work where a final decree isn't available yet. I'll tell you your options.

Navigating a mortgage during divorce?

Send me your situation and any relevant court documents. I'll build a plan that coordinates your mortgage with your legal timeline.
