## SCENARIOS PAGE (expertise/)

### 01. First-Time Home Buyers
You've been told you need 20% down. You've been told you can't afford it. You've been told a dozen conflicting things by people who never bothered to run your actual numbers. I walk you through the entire process from pre-approval to keys, explain every cost in plain English, and make sure you're not leaving money on the table with programs you didn't know existed.

### 02. Self-Employed & Business Owners
Your business does well. Your tax returns say otherwise because your accountant is doing their job. Most lenders look at your Schedule C or K-1 and panic. I know how to read the real story in your docs. The add-backs, the depreciation, the distributions your 1040 doesn't show. Your income is real. I know how to prove it.

### 03. Multiple Income Streams
Three 1099s, a side LLC, some rental income, and maybe a W-2 from two years ago you left behind. Most Austin mortgage lenders see this and close the file. I add it all up, figure out which income sources each lender will count, and match you with the one that qualifies the most of it. Variable income is not a disqualifier. It just takes a broker who knows how to document it.

### 04. 1099 / Freelance / Gig Workers
Three 1099s, a side business, and some contract work that pays different amounts every month. Most lenders see that and close the file. I see it and start adding. If you earn the money, I can find the right way to document it and the right lender to approve it.

### 05. Investor Scaling a Portfolio
You own four rental properties and want a fifth but your Schedule E is a mess and lenders keep rejecting you because of reported losses that aren't real losses. I know how to read Schedule E the way it's supposed to be read. Add back the depreciation, normalize the vacancy, show the lender your portfolio actually cash flows. I help investors go from five properties to twenty by restructuring what they own so they can keep acquiring.

### 06. Recently Changed Jobs or Employment Gaps
You just started a new role, switched industries, or have a gap on your resume that makes mortgage lenders in Austin nervous. There are ways to qualify with an offer letter, with less than two years at your current job, even during a probationary period. Most lenders default to no. I figure out which ones say yes and under what conditions.

### 07. Credit Score on the Edge
You're 15 points below where you need to be and you don't know what moves the needle fastest. I pull your credit, run a rapid rescore simulation, and tell you exactly what to pay off, dispute, or restructure to hit your target score. Sometimes it takes a week. Sometimes one balance paydown changes everything. I've gotten borrowers mortgage-ready in days when other lenders told them to wait six months.

### 08. Gift Funds or Hard-to-Source Assets
Your parents are giving you money for the down payment. You're sitting on crypto gains. You sold a car three months ago and the deposit looks weird on your bank statement. Every dollar in your account at closing needs a paper trail and most first-time home buyers in Texas don't know that until it's a problem. I know exactly what the sourcing requirements are for every program and I'll tell you how to document it before it becomes an issue.

### 09. Divorced, Separated, or Buying Off a Co-Borrower
You need to refinance your ex off the mortgage. Or you're buying a new place in Austin and your old debt still shows on your credit. Or there's a court order involved and your lender doesn't know how to read it. I deal with divorce-related mortgage files regularly. The decree, the quit claim, the liability exclusions, the timing. I know how all of it works.

### 10. Tight Timeline, Nobody Else Can Close
Your contract closes in two weeks and your current lender just fell apart. Or you found the right house and the seller wants to close fast. My fastest clear-to-close was 5 days from application. I move fast because I'm the only person touching your file and I don't wait on other departments to do their jobs. If your Austin home purchase can be done, I'll get it done.

### 11. Complex Income
K-1 partnership distributions. RSU and stock compensation. Commission that swings 40% year to year. Bonus income your employer structures differently every quarter. Austin is full of tech workers and entrepreneurs whose income is real but doesn't fit in a box. I know how every lender underwrites these sources and I match you with the one that counts the most of it.

### 12. Credit Rebuilds & Past Events
Bankruptcy. Foreclosure. Short sale. Collections you forgot about. A rough couple of years that left marks on your credit. Every program has waiting periods and minimum score thresholds, and I know exactly what they are. If you're past the waiting period, I can get you into a loan. If you're not there yet, I'll tell you exactly what to do and when to come back.

## PROGRAMS PAGE (programs/)

### Conventional
The standard. If you have solid credit and some money to put down, this is probably where you land. Conventional loans offer the best rates in most scenarios and the most flexibility on property types. It's the workhorse of mortgage lending for a reason. I price these across my full lender network to make sure you're not leaving money on the table.

### FHA
FHA exists for a reason. Flexible credit requirements, low down payment options, and qualifying ratios that give you room to breathe. Built for first-time buyers and anyone who needs a wider path to approval. If another lender told you your credit isn't good enough or your debt is too high, there's a good chance they just didn't want to do the work. I do.

### VA
Zero down. No PMI. The single best mortgage product that exists. If you served, this benefit is yours and you should use it. I know VA loans inside and out. Entitlement calculations, funding fee exemptions, using your benefit a second or third time, buying with partial entitlement. Most lenders fumble VA files because they don't do enough of them. I do plenty.

### USDA
Zero down for eligible rural areas. And a lot of areas near Austin qualify that people don't expect. Bastrop, Elgin, Liberty Hill, parts of Hutto, Wimberley, and more. I check USDA eligibility on every property before we go down this road. If your area qualifies, this is one of the best deals in lending. If it doesn't, I'll find the next best option.

### Jumbo
Above $832,750 in Texas, you're in jumbo territory. Westlake, Tarrytown, Lakeway, Barton Creek, Lost Creek. Jumbo underwriting is pickier, but the rates are more competitive than most people expect. I work with lenders who specialize in jumbo pricing and I know what underwriters at that level want to see. If you're buying or refinancing at scale, I handle these regularly.

### Self-Employed / Bank Statement
Your business does well but your tax returns don't show it because your accountant did their job. Bank statement loans skip the tax returns entirely and qualify you based on 12 to 24 months of deposits. I calculate your qualifying income from your actual cash flow, not your adjusted gross income. If you've been told you don't make enough on paper, this is probably the fix.

### DSCR / Investment Property
DSCR loans qualify based on the rental property's income, not yours. No tax returns. No W-2s. No explaining every LLC you own. If the rent covers the mortgage payment, you're in the conversation. I work with investors buying their second property and investors buying their twentieth. Same process, same attention, same phone number.

### Refinance (Rate and Term)
You locked a rate when you had no choice and now you want to fix it. Or your financial picture has changed and you want better terms. I'll pull your current loan details, run the numbers, and tell you straight whether a refinance saves you money. If it does, we move. If it doesn't, I'll tell you exactly what rate you'd need to hit before it makes sense.

### Cash-Out Refinance
You've built equity. Now you want to use it. Renovations, paying off high-interest debt, funding a business, buying another property. I'll show you how much you can pull, what it does to your payment, and whether the math actually works in your favor. Not every cash-out makes sense. I'll tell you if yours does or doesn't.

### Construction Loans
Financing a new build from the ground up. Construction-to-permanent loans start as a construction line, fund the build through a draw schedule, then convert to a permanent mortgage when the house is done. One closing instead of two. I manage the draw schedule, coordinate with your builder and the lender, and keep the timeline on track so your project doesn't stall waiting on funding.

### Renovation Loans (FHA 203k)
Buy a fixer and roll the renovation costs into one loan. Finance the purchase and the rehab together instead of buying the house, then scrambling to find a second loan for the work. I handle the contractor bids, the inspections, and the draw process. These loans have more moving parts than a standard purchase. That's exactly why you want one person managing the whole thing.

### Non-QM / Alternative Documentation
For borrowers who don't fit the standard boxes. Asset depletion for retirees with wealth but no traditional income. Foreign national buyers. ITIN borrowers. Interest-only structures. Recent credit events with short waiting periods. If a conventional or government loan doesn't work for your situation, there is almost always another path. I know where to find it and which lenders will actually close it.

### HomeReady (Fannie Mae)
Fannie Mae's HomeReady program is built for buyers whose income falls below the area median. It allows flexible sources for your down payment, including gifts, grants, and Community Seconds. You can add boarder income or bring in a non-occupant co-borrower to strengthen your file. I match HomeReady loans to the lender in my network that prices them best, because not every lender offers the same deal on these.

### HomePossible (Freddie Mac)
Freddie Mac's version of an affordable lending program. Similar intent to HomeReady but with its own set of guidelines, and sometimes one prices better than the other depending on your profile. Flexible income sourcing and qualifying rules that give first-time buyers and lower-income borrowers more room. I run both programs side by side and tell you which one wins for your situation.

### Adjustable Rate Mortgages (ARMs)
Not for everyone. But sometimes the smartest play on the board. If you know you're selling or refinancing within a few years, an ARM gives you a lower initial rate and saves you real money during that window. I'll show you exactly where the adjustment happens, what caps are in place, and what the worst-case scenario looks like so you can decide with your eyes open. No surprises.

### Bridge Loans
Short-term financing for investors who need to move fast on a property before another one sells or stabilizes. Bridge loans cover the gap so you're not waiting on a sale to close on your next deal. No contingencies, no stalling. I coordinate the timing between transactions so the money lands where it needs to when it needs to. When the exit property closes or refinances, you pay off the bridge and keep moving.

### FHA Streamline Refinance
Already have an FHA loan? You can drop your rate with minimal paperwork and no appraisal in most cases. The whole point of a streamline is speed. Less documentation, faster timeline, lower cost to close. I verify that the numbers actually improve your situation before we move. If the savings aren't real, I'll tell you to wait.

### VA IRRRL (Streamline Refinance)
The VA's Interest Rate Reduction Refinance Loan is one of the simplest refinances in the industry. If you already have a VA loan, you can lower your rate with minimal documentation and no appraisal. I make sure the net tangible benefit is there before we start the file. If it doesn't save you money, there's no reason to do it. When it does, these close fast.

### Debt Consolidation Refinance
Credit cards, car notes, student loans, medical bills. If you're sitting on equity and making five different payments at five different rates, consolidating them into your mortgage can cut your total monthly outflow significantly. I run the full comparison so you can see exactly what changes. This isn't about hiding debt. It's about restructuring it so it actually works for you.

### Interest-Only Loans
For investment properties where you want to keep your monthly carry as low as possible during a hold period. You pay only interest for a set period, which maximizes cash flow while you stabilize, renovate, or wait for the right time to sell or refinance. I walk you through the full amortization schedule so you understand exactly what happens when the interest-only period ends. If it fits your investment strategy, I'll find the best lender for it.

### Asset Depletion
You have the money. You just don't have a paycheck. Retirees, high-net-worth individuals, anyone living off investments or savings. Asset depletion takes your liquid assets and converts them into qualifying monthly income without requiring you to actually liquidate anything. I know which lenders accept this and how they calculate it, because the methodology varies and it matters.

### Foreign National Loans
Non-US citizens buying property in Texas. No Social Security number required. Different documentation, different down payment expectations, and a smaller pool of lenders who actually do these. I know which ones do, how they underwrite, and what they need from you upfront so we don't waste time on a lender who can't close it.

### ITIN Loans
For borrowers with an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number instead of a Social Security number. This is a legitimate path to homeownership and there are lenders who specialize in it. I know the programs, the documentation requirements, and which lenders actually close these files. If you have an ITIN and want to buy a home in Texas, I can help.

### Home Equity / Second Lien
You want cash but you don't want to give up the rate on your first mortgage. A second lien lets you tap your equity through a separate loan in second position. You keep your existing mortgage exactly where it is. I run the math on second lien versus cash-out refi for every client who asks, because sometimes one makes more sense than the other. I'll tell you which.
