RECENT CREDIT EVENT

A rough chapter. Not the last one.

Every program has waiting periods after bankruptcy, foreclosure, or short sale. I know exactly what they are. If you're past yours, we move forward. If not, I'll tell you when.

Overview

Businesses fail. Medical emergencies drain accounts. Marriages dissolve and take finances down with them. A credit event means you're on a timeline. It doesn't mean the door is permanently closed.

Every loan program has specific waiting periods after major events. The length depends on the type of event and the loan program. Some are measured in years. Non-QM lenders with specialized programs can work much sooner.

I know every waiting period for every combination. I'll tell you exactly where you are, what you qualify for today, and what opens up next. If you need a credit rebuilding strategy for the interim, I help with that too.

Key Details

WAITING PERIODS VARY
Each combination of event and program has its own timeline. Some require years. Non-QM options can be available shortly after discharge.
EXTENUATING CIRCUMSTANCES
Some programs allow shorter waits when the event was caused by a documented one-time occurrence beyond your control.
CREDIT REBUILDING
During the waiting period, I help you build a strategy to maximize your credit position for when you're eligible.
NON-QM PATH
Specialized lenders work with borrowers shortly after credit events. Higher rates, but available when traditional programs aren't.

How I Handle This

I understand the event. Type, date, discharge status. Map it against every program's requirements. Show you what's available now and what opens up at each future milestone.

If you're eligible today, we move. If not, I build a plan and follow up when your window opens.

Questions I Get

How soon after bankruptcy?

Depends on the chapter and the program. Non-QM may be available much sooner. I map your specific timeline.

Chapter 7 vs Chapter 13?

Different waiting periods. Chapter 13's repayment structure sometimes leads to shorter waits.

What about extenuating circumstances?

Medical emergency, involuntary job loss, natural disaster. If documented, some programs reduce the waiting period. I help determine if you qualify.

What should I do while waiting?

Build clean payment history. Keep utilization low. Avoid new derogatory items. Save for a down payment. I give you specific targets.

Past a credit event and ready to move forward?

Tell me what happened and when. I'll tell you where you stand today and when each program opens up.
