## REG Z TRIGGER TERMS — CRITICAL

If ANY of these appear in content, full TILA disclosures (APR, repayment terms, down payment) are REQUIRED. On the homepage and marketing pages, DO NOT USE TRIGGER TERMS. Save them for interior service pages with proper disclosure tables.

### TRIGGER TERMS (never use without full disclosures):
- Any specific interest rate: "6.1%", "5.99%", "rates starting at 6%"
- Any specific down payment amount or percentage: "3% down", "3.5% down", "$10,000 down"
- Any specific monthly payment: "$2,800/month", "$1,650 per month"
- Any specific loan term: "30-year fixed", "15-year mortgage"
- Any specific finance charge: "$500 total cost", "2 points"
- Any rate + term combo: "6.1% on a 30-year" (double trigger)
- Credit score minimums tied to programs: "580 credit score for FHA" (implies specific terms)

### SAFE LANGUAGE (use freely):
- "Competitive rates" / "Low rates" / "Great rates"
- "Affordable payments" / "Flexible terms"
- "Low down payment options available"
- "Fast closings" / "Apply today"
- "FHA/VA/Conventional loans available"
- "Contact us for a quote"
- "Flexible credit requirements"
- "Zero down" for VA (this is a program feature, not a trigger term when stated alone)
- "No PMI" for VA (program feature)
- Stating the conforming loan limit ($832,750) is informational, not a trigger
- Median home prices, days on market, inventory stats are informational

### IF YOU MUST USE TRIGGER TERMS:
Per §1026.24(e), on websites, trigger term disclosures may appear on a separate /disclosures page IF the trigger term clearly links (hyperlink) to that page. The disclosure page must include:
1. The amount or percentage of down payment
2. Full terms of repayment over the entire loan term
3. The APR, using the term "Annual Percentage Rate" spelled out
4. Whether the APR may increase after consummation
5. The APR must be displayed with EQUAL OR GREATER prominence than the interest rate

### RATE DISPLAY RULES:
- An interest rate may NEVER be displayed in a larger font than the APR
- If showing a rate, the APR must appear immediately adjacent, same font size
- Rate effective date must be included: "Rates as of [date]"
- "Subject to change without notice" disclaimer required
- If multiple rates apply over the loan term, show ALL rates, ALL periods, AND the APR

## LENDER-PAID COMPENSATION LANGUAGE

Case operates on lender-paid compensation. The borrower does not pay Case's fee.

### CORRECT language:
- "You do not pay my fee. The lender does."
- "No processing fees, no admin fees, no broker charges on your side"
- "My compensation comes from the lender, not from you"

### INCORRECT / RISKY language:
- "What I quote you is what you pay" (misleading if they don't pay the broker fee)
- "Free" (never describe mortgage services as free)
- "No cost" (implies no closing costs at all, which is false)
- Any language implying the borrower pays the broker directly

## LOAN COUNT / STATS CLAIMS

- "1,400+ loans closed by Kellibrooke" — MUST include "by Kellibrooke" since this is company-wide, not Case's personal count. Without the qualifier, it implies Case personally closed 1,400 loans, which is a UDAP (deceptive practices) exposure.
- "5 days fastest clear-to-close" — this is clear-to-close, NOT closing. Always specify "clear-to-close" not "closing"
- "21 days average closing" — verify this is accurate and document the methodology
- Any stat claim should be defensible if audited

## GLBA PRIVACY (Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act)

- Required if collecting ANY financial information via web forms (SSN, income, debt, employment, credit score, loan amount)
- Simple contact forms (name, email, phone only) may not trigger GLBA
- Privacy Policy page required, linked from site-wide footer
- Forms collecting financial data must include mandatory privacy notice acknowledgment checkbox
- Use CFPB Model Privacy Form as template (safe harbor)

## CONTENT TOPICS — SAFE vs NEEDS REVIEW

### SAFE (no compliance review needed):
- Market data (median prices, days on market, inventory levels)
- Educational content about the mortgage process
- Explanation of loan types (what FHA is, what VA is, etc.) without specific rates/terms
- Case's bio and experience
- General service area descriptions
- Client testimonials (if real and unedited)
- "How it works" process descriptions
- Calculator tools (if they don't pre-populate specific rates)

### NEEDS COMPLIANCE REVIEW:
- Any page with specific rates, payments, down payment percentages, or loan terms
- Rate comparison content ("my rate vs your bank's rate")
- Content referencing specific DPA dollar amounts tied to loan programs
- Social media ads (may have different disclosure requirements)
- Email marketing campaigns
- Any content making competitive claims ("lowest rates", "best in Austin")
- Print materials, flyers, or any offline advertising

### PROHIBITED:
- "Lowest rates guaranteed" without substantiation
- "No cost mortgage" or "free mortgage"
- "Government loan program" unless actually FHA/VA/USDA
- Using "counselor" (reserved for non-profit housing counselors)
- Making promises about approval ("you WILL be approved")
- Implying guaranteed outcomes
- Misleading use of "fixed" for variable-rate products
